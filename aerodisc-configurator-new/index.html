Heritage

100 Years of Wheel Covers

From Bugatti's first center-lock hub to Cybertruck's polygonal aero — a century of engineering shaped what your Aerodisc rolls on today.

1924
Bugatti Type 35

First race car with lightweight aluminum covers + center-lock hub. Pit stops dropped from 5 minutes to under one.

1930s
Cadillac & Packard

Full chrome hubcaps turned wheel covers into "moving jewelry" — function gave way to fashion and brand prestige.

1970s
Chevrolet Caprice

Injection-molded plastic put stylish, affordable wheel customization into every driveway in America.

1979
Porsche 935 · Ferrari 512 BBLM

Turbofan covers evacuated brake heat at Le Mans. Spinning at full throttle, they became icons of raw performance.

2017
Tesla Model 3 Aero

Functional aero caps returned to mass-market EVs, delivering a 2–4% range boost — and a debate about looks vs. miles.

2020
Koenigsegg Jesko

First hypercar to fuse center-lock hubs with carbon-fiber turbofans — engineering art at peak velocity.

2020
Bugatti Bolide

The first wheel cover born from the body itself — ducted, seamless, sculpted into the aero shell.

2021
McLaren Speedtail

Fully enclosed front covers tamed lift at near-400 km/h. The wheel didn't slice the air — it joined the sculpture.

2022
F1 Closed Hubs

Closed wheel hubs became regulation across all teams — once experimental, now codified into the sport.

2023
Tesla Cybertruck

Polygonal closed-face caps fused drag reduction with geometry-as-identity — a wheel cover that became a meme.

2024
Tesla Cybercab

Fully enclosed, lightweight covers engineered for autonomous fleet duty — function without fanfare.

2025
Aerodisc · The Cultural Apex

A civilian center-lock hub with interchangeable shells — Bugatti's 1924 race-tech, reimagined for personal expression.
